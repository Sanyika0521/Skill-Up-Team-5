Helping others is considered good for several reasons, both from moral and practical standpoints:

Empathy and Compassion: helping others reflects empathy and compassion, which are fundamental aspects of being human. It shows that you care about the well-being of others and can stranghten social bonds.

Sense of fulfillment: helping others often brings a sense of fulfillment and satisfaction. It can give you a purpose beyond your own needs and desires, contributing to a greater sense of happiness and well-being.

Altruism: engaging in acts of kindness and generosity without expecting anything in return is a demonstration of altruism. Altruistic behavior contributes to a more cohesive and supportive society.

Reciprocity: the principle of reciprocity suggests that when you help others, they are more likely to help you in return when you need it. This fosters mutual support networks and can create a cycle of generosity and kindness.

Social benefits: helping others can strenghten social connections and relationships. It builds trust and fosters a sense of community, which is beneficial for both individuals and society as a whole.

Psychological benefits: research has shown that helping others can have positive effects on mental health, reducing stress, anxiety, and depression. It can also enhance feelings of self-worth and purpose.

Contribution to society: by helping others, you contribute to the betterment of society as a whole. Whether trough volunteering, charitable donations, or simply lending a helping hand, your actions can make a positive impact on the world around you.
