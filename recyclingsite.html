Recycling is good for several reasons:


Conservation of Resources: Recycling helps conserve natural resources like timber, water, and minerals.
By reusing materials, we reduce the need to extract and process raw materials, which can be
environmentally damaging.

Reduction of Landfill Waste: Recycling reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills, which helps
mitigate pollution and environmental degradation. Landfills produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas,
and can contaminate soil and water.

Energy Savings: Recycling often requires less energy than producing new materials from raw resources.
For example, recycling aluminum saves around 95% of the energy required to produce aluminum from
its raw materials.

Reduced Pollution: Recycling reduces pollution associated with the extraction, transportation, and
processing of raw materials. It also decreases pollution from landfill sites and incineration.

Conservation of Habitat: By reducing the demand for raw materials, recycling helps protect natural
habitats from destruction due to mining, logging, and other extraction activities.

Economic Benefits: Recycling can create jobs and stimulate economic activity in industries related to
waste collection, processing, and manufacturing of recycled products.

Community Engagement: Recycling encourages communities to become more environmentally
conscious and engaged in sustainable practices, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the
environment.
